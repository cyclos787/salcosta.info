Game Design

Top–Down Dungeon Crawler

Game Description

A top-down style dungeon crawler with a dark ambience. The player is to escape the dungeon by collecting keys and defeating the boss, finding weapon upgrades and fighting enemies around the level. The map contains traps and several triggers to advance or alter the level. I worked on this game in a team of 4 students with 6 weeks to build the game.

Overview of level map

Features

  • 2 types of minion enemies and a boss enemy, with full combat system and different weapons
  • Original sound effects and music (found on Music page)
  • Interactive locking doors and switches to advance through game
  • Detailed level with occlusion effect making the player always visible through materials

Models for wall torch (left) and rubble pile (right)

My Roles

  • Composed original music and created sound effects
  • Programmed combat system; dealing and taking of damage with variables based on the types of weapon and enemy
  • Created menu screens and HUD layout, as well as UI graphics
  • Created models in Autodesk Maya for level props
  • Worked on traceability matrix and helped manage project direction

Takeaways

  • Gained experience working in a team setting with a limited timeline
  • Experienced the amount of interactions that can occur when multiple people are working with different coding styles/approaches
  • Enjoyed working in a team setting, communication was most important for the team's success

First–Person Treasure Hunter

Game Description

This game gives the player the task of collecting all 10 gemstones before time runs out. The player can “dig” the terrain to look beneath the surface for items, walking close to buried treasure gives the player a visual and audio cue to indicate where buried treasure lies.

View of items spawned beneath level surface

Features

  • Procedural terrain that can be lowered or raised by the player, with a specified dig radius
  • Item detection when player is near/above treasure
  • Randomly spawned items and time bonuses within diggable terrain area

Takeaways

  • Learned the fundamentals of having the player interact with different elements of the game world
  • Learned the amount of troubleshooting involved in game development, as this was my first Unreal project
  • Gained experience working with tick based and time based events
